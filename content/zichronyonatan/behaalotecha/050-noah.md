This week's parsha concludes with the paradigm of lashon hara -- Aharon and Miriam speaking about Moshe. This saga begins, "Vatedaber Miriam ve'Aharon..." -- "And Miriam and Aharon spoke against Moshe" -- and ends with Miriam struck with tzara'at, the entire camp waiting for her healing before continuing their journey.

That the whole nation would wait for one person's recovery -- even after a sin of speech -- speaks to the seriousness with which the Torah treats both the damage careless words can do, and the dignity owed to the person who caused it. Even a leader as consequential as Miriam is not abandoned by her community; she is waited for.
