Rivkah waited many years to have children. Yet when she finally conceived, she was distraught about some very peculiar behavior on her fetus' behalf. When she would walk by a study hall where Torah was learned the baby would excitedly kick and jump, as if pulled by the holiness of the place outside. Yet when she would pass by a house of idolatry the same thing would happen, demonstrating a deep desire for the evil found nearby. Not knowing what to make of such behavior, she went to a prophet to request an explanation. The answer she received was that this was not a single pregnancy but rather it was twins. One would be a great tzadik and one would be wicked. This explained the inconsistency – the righteous son was excited by Torah whereas the wicked son liked the hype of idolatry. This explanation calmed her and she was able to continue through the pregnancy, eventually giving birth to Yaakov and Esav.

What kind of consolation is that? Why was she suddenly so happy - wasn't there some very bad news here about the wicked son she was carrying?

In good Jewish style, let's answer that question with another. Yitro, the father-in-law of Moshe, had several name including the name Putiel. This name was given to him by virtue of the fact that, prior to discovering the truth of Hashem, he had fattened calves for idolatrous sacrifice. This is a hard explanation to swallow, because we have a rule of thumb that one may not remind a righteous person of his sinful past. Why, then, does the Torah refer to him with this seemingly disdainful name?

The answer is that Yitro did not only serve idols, but he fattened up the calves in order to serve them in the best way possible. Yitro was someone who gave his whole heart and soul to his beliefs. Yes, at the beginning his beliefs were misguided, but he left no stone unturned in his quest for the truth. Because of his complete dedication to the truth, he was inclined to fatten the calves for sacrifice – to serve those idols like no one ever did before. As he climbed to the top of each religion he eventually found its holes and that same dedication to the truth caused him to drop it all and search for truth elsewhere. It is this unbelievable character trait for which the Torah named him Putiel, because this was ultimately the reason he was able to finally discover the truth of Hashem.

In light of this explanation, we can understand why Rivkah was comforted with this seemingly ill report. Originally, she believed that she had only one baby and it was this same baby who seemed so drawn to Torah today and to evil tomorrow. This prospect disturbed her greatly because the incongruence demonstrated a total lack of dedication to the truth. Someone who can act one way today and the opposite way tomorrow is clearly unbeholden to any belief system. This apathy was something Rivkah could not bear because one who does not care for the truth is beyond help. Nothing she could do or say would ever make an impression on such a child. When she was told that this was in fact a double pregnancy and there were two different children with different leanings, this calmed her nerves. Now she understood that she would have a wicked son, but at least he was loyal. If he stood for his value system, however crooked it may be, there was hope of showing him the proper way and
bringing him back to the truth.

In our society, apathy reigns supreme. In years gone past in was not at all uncommon to convince somebody of the truth of Judaism and watch them change their entire lives in order to follow that truth. Uncle Yonatan was a case in point. Kiruv was largely an intellectual field, which aimed to show the truth of Torah through logical reasoning. Now, such arguments are almost futile – you can convince someone from today to tomorrow that this is the truth but the answer is simply, “Who cares?” What hope can there be for a generation that gives no value and no loyalty to whatever the truth may be?

Let us examine our own lives and strive to see to it that our actions and lifestyles are indeed congruent with the truths that we perceive. Let us dedicate ourselves to the pursuit of truth and thereby we too will find the correct way in every facet of our lives.
