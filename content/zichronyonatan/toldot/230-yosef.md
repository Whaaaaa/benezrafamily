Sometime last year, Elon Musk, the billionaire CEO of Tesla Motors, received a notice that his net worth had just jumped, establishing him as the wealthiest man on earth. Musk responded with a tweet that read, 'How interesting – well, back to work.' Despite my nausea with the fact that such a person is still not satisfied with his wealth, I think there is something for us to learn from this highly successful businessman. Although he is wasting his energies amassing things he will never even enjoy, it is the insatiable drive that has driven his success which we can use to fuel our everlasting spiritual endeavors.

When Eisav was born, everyone together called him Eisav (עשו), which connotes something complete and fully done – a finished product. The reason for this unanimous naming was because Eisav emerged from the womb in a mature state with hair like a full-grown man.

Yaakov, too, was named after the state of his birth. He was born tugging at the heel of his older twin in an effort to win himself the status of the firstborn, and he was thereby called Yaakov (יעקב) – a form of the Hebrew word for heel (עקב).

This is the simple understanding of their names, but there seems to be greater depth here. We know that a name has tremendous bearing on the entire essence of a person and his destiny in the world. It would serve us well to understand the deeper meaning of these names and how they are connected with the respective destinies of Yaakov and Eisav.

Rabbi Simcha Maimon explains that Eisav and Yaakov were not so very far from each other at birth. Eisav in his own way shared equally the potential to become one of the forefathers. His natural talents and internal strengths were suited no less for greatness than his brother Yaakov, although the battles he would need to overcome were different than the battles of Yaakov.

What then was the deciding factor which catapulted Yaakov to the highest levels man could ever reach, whereas Eisav sunk so low as to having earned the title 'Eisav the Wicked'?

It all lies in the name.

As mentioned, Eisav came into the world with everything going for him. The son of Yitzchak. The grandson of Avraham. One possessing almost super-human strength and intelligence. One gifted with a soul of unspeakable greatness. But Eisav viewed himself as depicted in his name – complete and perfect, lacking nothing and with no need to accomplish more. He wanted to ride the wave of his innate greatness without putting in the effort to accomplish more. In his book, he had it made. Eisav, a finished product.

Not so Yaakov. Yaakov found himself gifted with all the same greatness and God-given gifts, but never was he satisfied. With his first breath, Yaakov was already striving for more, clawing at his brother's heel in an effort to secure the spiritual status of the firstborn. Yaakov never stopped, never rested on his laurels, and never thought himself to be a finished product. From his formative years as the quintessential yeshiva bochur, never leaving the tent of Torah, to his superhuman efforts in winning Rochel's hand in marriage, to his eventual battle with Eisav's protecting angel – Yaakov never stopped fighting, striving, and accomplishing.

This is the secret which defined the destiny of the two brothers.

Life has been compared to a treadmill. If you try to stand still, you can rest assured that you're falling back. The idea that we can just sit tight and enjoy the levels which we have been gifted, or even those that we have previously earned, is nothing but a fantasy. When Eisav tried to coast on the gifts of his birthright, he ultimately lost it all, falling even lower than one not possessed of those gifts could have ever fallen. Yaakov, on the other hand, started in the clouds but never stopped reaching for the stars. Ultimately, he rose to indescribable heights – to the point that his picture is depicted on the heavenly throne.

Sometimes we see two people who, in their youth, were more or less on equal footing and headed more or less in the same direction. Yet if you meet them years down the road you sometimes see a tremendous gap between them. One has far outdone his peer. This happens in many arenas, be it in spiritual growth or in corporate success. Very often, the differences between them are just like the differences between Yaakov and Eisav. One of them looked at his past accomplishments, patted himself on the back, and tried to ride the wave, whereas the other looked only forward. The one who thought to take easy street is ultimately left empty-handed, while his friend strives ever higher, never content with past achievements.

So let's take the example of those who know how to achieve success. Not just the Musk's of the world, who have shown what mettle it takes to make it but waste the effort on material pursuits, but the Yaakov Avinu's who have shown us what true accomplishment is and the key to getting there.
