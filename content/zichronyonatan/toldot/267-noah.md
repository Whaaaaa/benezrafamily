And Esau said, “I am going to die, so of what use is my birthright to me?”
וַיֹּ֣אמֶר עֵשָׂ֔ו הִנֵּ֛ה אָנֹכִ֥י הוֹלֵ֖ךְ לָמ֑וּת וְלָמָּה־זֶּ֥ה לִ֖י בְּכֹרָֽה׃

This moment when Esau agrees to sell Yaakov the birthright sets the stage for the most severe familial split in the Torah. What was Esau’s reasoning?

The posuk above provides an insight into his decision-making process. Esau’s anticipation of his own death is apparently the factor that made him acquiesce to Yaakov’s offer.

The Gemara in Sotah makes reference to Esau’s attitude in a very different context. While Pharaoh's daughter walked along the riverside to retrieve Moshe, the Torah tells us, “her maidens walked along.” The Gemara says that the word “walking” (holech) in regard to Pharaoh’s maidservants alludes to impending death just as Esau said, “I am going (holech) to die.”

ונערותיה הולכות וגו' א"ר יוחנן אין הליכה זו אלא לשון מיתה וכן הוא אומר (בראשית כה, לב) הנה אנכי הולך למות
The verse continues: “And her maidens walked along [holekhot] by the riverside” (Exodus 2:5). Rabbi Yoḥanan says: This walking is nothing other than the terminology of going toward death, and similarly it states: “Behold, I am going [holekh] to die” (Genesis 25:32).
שמות ב, ה: ותרא את התיבה בתוך הסוף כיון דחזו דקא בעו לאצולי למשה אמרו לה גבירתנו מנהגו של עולם מלך בשר ודם גוזר גזירה אם כל העולם כולו אין מקיימין אותה בניו ובני ביתו מקיימין אותה ואת עוברת על גזירת אביך בא גבריאל וחבטן בקרקע
The verse continues: “And she saw the ark among the willows” (Exodus 2:5). Once her maidens saw that the daughter of Pharaoh was intending to save Moses, they said to her: Our mistress, the custom of the world is that when a king of flesh and blood decrees a decree, even if all the world does not fulfill it, at least his children and members of his household fulfill it, and yet you are violating the decree of your father. After the maidens tried to convince her not to save Moses, the angel Gabriel came and beat them to the ground and they died.

The Gemara is drawing a connection between the death delivered to Pharaoh's-daughter’s naysaying-maidservants and Esau’s mindset. What links these events? Esau would continue to live for years after he claimed, “I am going to die,” while the maidservants didn’t make it through the afternoon. What links these events and why was Esau so certain that he would die?

Rashi explains:
אָמַר עֵשָׂו: מַה טִּיבָהּ שֶׁל עֲבוֹדָה זוֹ? אָמַר לוֹ: כַּמָּה אַזְהָרוֹת וָעֳנָשִׁין וּמִיתוֹת תְּלוּיִין בָּהּ כְּאוֹתָהּ שֶׁשָּׁנִינוּ, אֵלּוּ הֵן: שְׁתוּיֵי
יַיִן וּפְרוּעֵי רֹאשׁ; אָמַר: אֲנִי הוֹלֵךְ לָמוּת עַל יָדָהּ, אִם כֵּן מַה חֵפֶץ לִי בָהּ שֶׁבְּמִיתָה
Esau said, “What is the nature of this Service?” Jacob replied, “Many prohibitions and punishments and many acts involving even the punishment of death are associated with it — just as we read in the Mishna, (Sanhedrin 22b): The following priests are liable to death: those who carry out their duties after having drunk too much wine and those who officiate long-haired. He said: If I am going to die through it, why should I desire it?

Even if the birthright and the accompanying temple service were valuable in Esau’s eyes, he was intimidated by the risk to his own life. He was too afraid to commit to something important because he couldn’t imagine giving himself over entirely to the cause. Contrast this with Pharoh’s daughter who likewise was warned about the riskiness of her actions. Her maidservants took Esau’s approach and deserted what was right in favor of the safe route.

What is the result of risking life itself for our greater values and morals? We see that Yaakov, who was willing to shoulder the responsibility of the temple service and Bas Pharoh who put her life on the line for Moshe are both granted eternal life.

The Gemara in Taanis says about Yaakov Avinu:
א"ר יוחנן יעקב אבינו לא מת
Rabbi Yochanan said, “Yaakov our father never died”

And in Derech Eretz Zuta among the 9 people who, “entered gan eden alive” we find Basya Bas Pharoh:
תשעה נכנסו בחייהם בגן עדן. ואלו הן: [...]ובתיה בת פרעה

When we elevate moral conviction above our material existence we can achieve immortality.
