On Lag Ba'Omer we celebrate the life of Rabbi Shimon Bar Yochai, a central contributor to the Mishnah and a devout student of Rabbi Akiva, with whom he studied Torah in defiance of Roman decree. After Rabbi Akiva's death and the death of many of his students, and after Roman restrictions eased, Rabbi Shimon and two colleagues discussed strategy toward the new regime -- one favoring friendliness, one neutral, and Rabbi Shimon, remembering his martyred colleagues, calling for maximum defiance. When this was reported to Rome, the friendly colleague became ambassador, and Rabbi Shimon was sentenced to death.

He and his son Rabbi Elazar hid in a cave for twelve years, sustained by carob and water, becoming great sages in isolation. When they finally emerged and saw fellow Jews plowing their fields, Rabbi Shimon exclaimed, "Imagine, giving up sacred Torah study for worldly matters!" -- and the fields they looked upon went up in smoke. A heavenly voice called out, "Have you come to destroy My world? Return to your cave!" They spent another twelve months there, undergoing what the tradition calls "an attitude adjustment."

When they finally re-emerged, they saw a Jew hurrying home with myrtle branches to make his house fragrant for Shabbat, and Rabbi Shimon, moved, told his son, "See how precious the mitzvot are to our brethren." Despite Roman persecution, ordinary Jews still found ways to celebrate and identify openly.

Later, seeing his poor health, a colleague wept for him; Rabbi Shimon reassured him that he never would have reached such heights of piety without his years in the cave. He went on to teach widely, and when persecution resumed, he led a delegation to the Roman emperor, healed the emperor's ill daughter, and was granted the power to end the decrees against the Jews.

Despite his years as a recluse, Rabbi Shimon is remembered above all for his ethics: "Man should rather jump into a fiery furnace than offend another in public." "To deceive anyone by words is worse than cheating him out of money." "He who lets arrogance get the better of him is like the heathen worshipping idols." Good Shabbos!
