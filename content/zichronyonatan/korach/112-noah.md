Rabbi Elazar Ha-kappar said: envy, lust and [the desire for] honor put a man out of the world.

Korach incites a mutiny against Moshe for granting his brother Aharon the priesthood. Together with Moshes' inveterate foes Datan and Aviram and another 250 distinguished members of the community they offer a sacrifice of incense to Hashem to prove their worthiness for the priesthood. The earth swallows the leaders and those offering the ketoret (incense) are consumed by fire.

While this incident may seem like a unique occurence in our peoples history the Shelah HaKadosh reveals a much deeper historical pattern that runs through this event. He teaches that this conflict stretches back to the first two brothers, Kayin and Hevel.

According to Rav Huna in the Midrash (Bereshis Rabbah 22:7) what were they [Kayin and Hevel] arguing about? R' Huna said: an extra twin sister was born with Hevel. This one said: I will take her because I am the first born. The other one said: I will take her because she was born with me. Because of this "…Kayin rose up against Hevel his brother and slew him."

Referencing this interpretation of the original conflict between brothers, the Shelah explains that Kayin had to go through three separate incarnations to recitfy the three fundamental spiritual components of a human, his נפש, his רוח, and his נשמה. The three incarnations are, the Egyptian that Moshe slayed, Yitro - Moshes' father in law and Korach.

The first incarnation was retribution for Kayin's killing of his brother out of lust and envy. Rashi teached us that the Egyptian Moshe saw brutally beating a slave was motivated out of lust for the man's wife. Here, Moshe in his capacity as Hevel's incarnation was able to carry out divine justice by killing the Egyptian the same way he had been struck down by his brother. The Shelah explains that this is hinted at in the verse ויך את המצרי. The numerical value of ויך, when you add one digit for the word itself, equals the numerical value of הבל, i.e. 37. The word המצרי, equals the numerical value of משה, i.e. 345.

The next incarnation was as Yitro. Yitro deferred to Moses by giving him his daughter Tzipporah as a wife. The Shelah explains She was the reincarnation (גלגול) of the extra twin that had been born with Hevrl, on whose account Kayin had slain Hevel out of jealousy. By giving her over to Moshe, here too divine justice is served.

Finally we have the namesake of our parsha. Korach now started a quarrel as he simply reverted to the pattern in which his original ancestor Kayin had acted. Moses then killed him (i.e. caused his death), fulfilling the Torah's commandment that if someone has shed innocent blood, his own blood will be spilled by human hand. We must understand that verse is telling us that the very person who had been slain, will in due course slay his murderer. This is why we find Moshe, who was in reality the re-incarnation of Hevel, slaying Korach who was the re-incarnation of Kayin.

How do these incarnations differ from eachother? It seems that each one moves deeper toward the underlying motivation that caused the original conflict between the brothers. The violent outcome is fixed by slaying the Egyptian, the lust and jealousy that drove Kayin to kill Hevel is addressed by Yitro giving his daughter to Moshe. What does Korach teach us about the core catalyst that caused the calamity?

After Kayin's offering is denied by Hashem, according to Targum Yonatan, Kayin came to the conclusion that, "There is no Judge or Law." This was the final justification that enabled him to commit fratricide. Korach represents this same ultimate rejection of a divine will. The Shelah explains, "Korach also denied the prophetic powers of Moses, that the Torah originated in Heaven and that the earth had been created because of the merit of Moses, as we have been told בראשית, בשביל משה, שנאמר וירא ראשית לו"

While Kayin's jealousy and lust had already been rectified through the previous lives, the ultimate denial of a creator and orchestrator was the final thread that had to be mended. The Shelah explains that this is alluded to through the juxtaposition of Korach's uprising and the Mitzvah of Tzitzit.

"The reason that the Torah reports the quarrel of Korach immediately after having taught us the laws about ציצית is explained by Rashi, who says that ציצית teaches the unity of G–d, His existence, and that He created the world ex nihilo. It also teaches that the origin of Torah is in Heaven, that it is not man made. It teaches that there is a system of reward and punishment which operates in this world. Concerning ציצית the Torah says "when you see Him i.e. אותו, etc." There are eight threads above the blue thread and eight threads below the blue thread which is threaded through the corner of the garment. Subsequently we attach five knots, each one being a double knot making a total of ten. Thus we arrive at a total of sixteen threads and ten knots, the numerical value of the Ineffable Name of God. This name teaches that He is, was, and will be, and that He is the Cause of all Existence.

It this understanding that allows us to remain in the world. The real world, the world with meaning and consequences and justice. Without it we are victims to lust, jealousy and the endless pursuit of honor. With this understanding we can come closer to emulating Moshe and eventually inheriting the world to come.
