On Shavuot we celebrate the day Hashem gave the Torah to Israel, in favor of every other nation of the world. The Midrash tells us this was not an arbitrary choice -- every other nation was offered the Torah first, and each declined once they learned what it would require of them. Only Israel answered, famously, na'aseh v'nishma -- "we will do, and we will listen" -- accepting before even hearing the terms.

There is a profound difference between a people who accept a covenant because it serves them, and a people who accept it before knowing what it will cost. It is this posture -- not calculating victimhood or advantage, but a willingness to commit first and understand later -- that the Midrash suggests made Israel, and Israel alone, fit to receive the Torah at all.

Shabbat Shalom
