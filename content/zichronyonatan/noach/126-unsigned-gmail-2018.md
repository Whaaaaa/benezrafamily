Noach and his family lived shortly after the creation of the world, in an era consumed by crime and violence. God instructs Noah to build an ark because he wants to drown the world but let some cream rise to the top. Over the course of the next 120 years, Noach builds a great big ark in his front yard. All the meanwhile his neighbors watch, making fun of him and skeptically hoping they would not get stuck in the rain. Although God made it so a lifetime could pass before the winds and rain started nobody headed Noachs warning and nobody repented. Noach was instructed to gather animals two by two for reproduction in the future after the flood. Noach's wife and family sealed themselves inside the ark and for forty days and nights, their vessel rose over the mountains until the earth could not be seen and all inhabitants man, beast and fowl were consumed by deep waters.

For a hundred and fifty days the ark bobbed lazily into decent where it settled on mount Arat. Noach dispatched a raven and multiple doves out across the water, one solar year from their arrival at mount Arat one dove returns with an olive branch. Noach is instructed to open the arc and populate the soil. Noah makes an offering to god and god promises by the sign of a rainbow that he will never drown the globe again. Noach grows a vineyard, and settles into the family life, drunkenly embarrassing his children. For ten generations Noach's children speak one language and live in a shared culture until their dispersal. With epic cooperation, they plan and attempt to build a structure that elevates them to the level of God. God floods their minds with babel and Noach's lineage is dispersed into seventy nations each with unique language and culture. This summarizes the time between Noach and Abram.

Rainbows have a very distinct symbolism in Judaism, though we are instructed to make a blessing after seeing one we are told not to see them, not to look at them at all. What kind of opportunity is this, one of blessing or one that reminds us of past crime resulting in global disaster?

A Rainbow is a sign that we are sinning, however, there have been generations that did not see a rainbow. The rule of King Chizkiyahu, the era of the Men of the Great Assembly, the generation of Rabbi Shimon bar Yochai and the generation of Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi. When this happens it means someone on the earth is so righteous that he acts as a foundation stone for the world. In a conversation between Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi and Rabbi Shimon bar Yochai in Gan Eden, Rabbi Shimon asked Rabbi Yehoshua if the rainbow had been seen in his lifetime. When Rabbi Yehoshua modestly hid his greatness by saying that it had, Rabbi Shimon said, “Then you’re not ben Levi!” the lesson is some greatness hides and some greatness can’t help it. When acting behind the scenes we have more room for error. When we step into the spotlight because we can't help it we will undoubtedly take a blow to our pride. This is a rare time god seemingly acts beyond his intent.

"And the Lord repented that He had made Man on the earth, and it grieved Him in His heart. And the Lord said, "I will destroy man whom I have created from the face of the earth, both man and beast, and the creeping thing and the fowls of the air, for I repent that I have made them."

This quote from before the flood shows Hashem having regret for creating all of man. How can god have grief or regret if he has already seen the show? He is beyond time, He knows the end before the beginning! The Midrash on these verses tells a story. A heretic asked R. Joshua ben Korchah: "Don't you Jews say that G‑d knows the future?"

Rabbi Joshua answered, "Yes."

"Why then," continued the heretic, "is it written 'grieved Him in His heart?"

Responded R. Joshua, "Was a son ever born to you?"

"Yes," said the heretic.

"What did you do?"

"I rejoiced." "But didn't you know that one day he will die?"

Replied the man, "One rejoices when it is a time for rejoicing, and one mourns when it is a time for mourning."Said R. Joshua, "So it is with G‑d.

Rashi adds "Although it was known to Him that they will sin and be destroyed, He nevertheless created them for the sake of the righteous who will descend from them."

When God chooses to perform miracles or permanently alter the world we live in he finds extreme measure. It takes many minor to add up to a flood of destruction. The bracha for seeing a rainbow is Blessed are You, Lord our G‑d, King of the universe, who remembers the covenant, and is faithful to His covenant, and keeps His promise.

The rainbow arc is the same arc as a bow and arrow. Stretched over the horizon the arrow would load facing the heavens. A sign of forgiveness and weapons at rest.

A rainbows main meaning is that although we have been treated harshly in the past we recognize we are the produce that lived through a harsh weathering. It is impossible for us to revert to bad ways as it is impossible for God to flood the earth again. The rainbow stands testimony to renewal, healthy only to those who are established enough to grow.

Some have found the rainbow to have different signs The prophet Ezekiel described a vision in which he had seen the divine presence “like a rainbow in the clouds on a rainy day, with a corona around it…” in response some said it would even be appropriate to bow when seeing a rainbow. Yet it is still impolite to stare, as it is Hashem's glory. The Zohar says its a sign of the era of Moshiach.
