Noach is a patriarch that we can all emulate. In this weeks Parsha Rashi tells us that Noach had doubts about the flood and never entered until it started raining. Noach is not credited with birthing a nation, or leading his people to the promised land. Unlike Moshe and Avraham Noach remains an unsung hero of humanity, and not just humans but life on earth.

Noach's strengths are in surviving and persevering through harsh criticism and doubt. Noach's super power is not letting even his own doubts distract him from the job. Noach's job was not pretty and much of his day on the ark was devoted to tending to animals.

There is a Yiddish saying Fun a kasha shtarbt men nit -- "Nobody ever died of a question." This saying is coming to teach us that while we are torn or doubtful we cannot become paralyzed. Noach was faced by the doubts of all his neighbors and he had his own doubts but the stakes were too high not to act.

The lesson of Parsha Noach is one of survival and perseverance through criticism and doubt. Noach's people speak one language and belong to one culture for ten generations until his descendents build a monument to their own invincibility. This is their ultimate downfall as God is forced to split their tongue and scatter them throughout the land. Noach's gift of action in face of doubt is a double edged sword but a gift nonetheless. Assertive people bring life into a room with them. Noach's ability to be assertive saved humanity.
