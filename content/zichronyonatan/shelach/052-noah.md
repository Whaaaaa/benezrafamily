The story of the spies is one of the most painful in the history of our people. We stood on the brink of entering the Holy Land, still under the leadership of Moshe, with G-d's promises fresh and undeniable -- and still, ten of the twelve spies convinced an entire generation to despair rather than trust. The tragedy of Shelach is not that the land was difficult, but that a people who had witnessed the sea split chose fear over the evidence of their own eyes.
