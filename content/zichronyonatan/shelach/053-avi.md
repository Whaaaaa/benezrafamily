In this week's Parsha Shelach, Moses sends a representative from each tribe of Israel into the land of Cannan. After a forty day expedition through the land the twelve men returned with a giant cluster of grapes and one oversized pomegranate. The land of Cannan was lush and bountiful! Ten of the men warned that the land was as dangerous as it was bountiful, saying that the inhabitants of the land were giants and warriors. Only Caleb and Joshua believed the land could be settled as Moshe instructed; the rest of the spies all believed that the Cannanites were stronger and more powerful than them.

The people of Israel wept that they wanted to return to Egypt so God decreed that they would not enter the holy land for forty years. In response some Jews stormed the border only to be met by Amalekites and Canaanites on all sides.

The laws of Nesachim are given in this Parsha. These are the laws regarding meal, wine and oil offerings. We are also given the Mitzvah of Challah, removing a portion of bread for sacrifice. At the end of the Parsha we are told the story of a wood gatherer who desecrated Shabbos and was put to death. We are commanded to wear tzitzit on our garments to remind us of all the Mitzvahs constantly.
