In this week's Parsha, Moshe sends a representative from each tribe of Israel to scout the land of Canaan. After forty days, the twelve men return bearing a giant cluster of grapes and an oversized pomegranate -- the land is lush and bountiful. Ten of the twelve warn that the land is as dangerous as it is bountiful, its inhabitants giants and warriors; only Calev and Yehoshua believe the land can be settled as Moshe instructed.

The people weep, wishing to return to Egypt, and G-d decrees they will not enter the land for forty years. Some Jews then storm the border regardless, only to be defeated by Amalekites and Canaanites.

The laws of the wine, oil, and flour libations are given, along with the mitzvah of Challah. The Parsha closes with the story of a man executed for gathering wood on Shabbat, and the commandment to wear tzitzit as a constant reminder of the mitzvot.

Good Shabbos, Avi
