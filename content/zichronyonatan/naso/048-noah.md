"May Hashem bless you and safeguard you. May Hashem light up His face to you and be gracious to you. May Hashem lift His face to you and give you peace." These words -- the Birkat Kohanim, the Priestly Blessing -- open this week's Parsha's most enduring legacy, still recited over children and congregations every week, millennia after they were first given to Aharon and his sons.

Naso is the longest Parsha in the Torah, filled with the meticulous, individualized offerings of each tribal leader at the Mishkan's dedication -- each essentially identical, yet each recorded in full, teaching that even a repeated, seemingly redundant act of devotion is worthy of being fully seen and named in its own right.

Shabbat Shalom
