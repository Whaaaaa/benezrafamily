Bechukotai, the second of this week's double parsha, opens with: "Im bechukotai teileichu ve'et mitzvotai tishmeru va'asitem otam" -- "If you walk in My statutes and keep My commandments and perform them." Rashi famously asks why "walking in My statutes" is needed at all once "keeping My commandments" is already stated -- and answers that this refers to diligent Torah study, the constant, active walking down the paths of Torah, not merely the passive performance of its commandments.

The blessings that follow -- rain in its season, produce in abundance, peace in the land -- are not a reward for observance alone, but specifically for the ongoing, walking engagement with Torah that keeps a person, and a community, alive and moving rather than static.
