Chanukah! A time for light! A time for family! Latkes, dreidels, and fire! Every pyros second favorite holiday (after lag baomer)!

But what is it really about?

One jug of oil that lasted for eight days? The Chashmonaim’s defeat of the Assyrian Greeks?

Uncle Yonatan was the first to teach me that the great sages of the time actually didn't establish the holiday until the year after the miracles. Strange that they had to wait a full year to give the Jewish people the most celebrated holiday even amongst the most secular Jews today.

What does Chanukah mean anyway?

Actually, the word Chanukah derives from the word chinuch, to train. It's the same word we use when referring to teaching children mitzvos. Only in this case, it is referring to “teaching” or “training” the Beis Hamikdash, the Holy Temple.

It’s a funny word to use really. To teach would be lilmod, from the root lomed, to learn. But chinuch refers to something else. To training something or someone into its full utilization of potential. If you ”taught” a child math, that would be lilmod, to teach. But when you teach a child mitzvos, that's chinuch, to train the child into his or her full utilization of potential.

This is why chinuch is something we daven, or pray, constantly to be able to give to our children. Teaching a subject is not nearly as difficult as being mechanech, the verb of chinuch.

In order to be mechanech, we must continually demonstrate how to connect to Hashem, we must take into consideration the age and level and even the unique attributes of the one we are training, and we must daven that Hashem bless our work and allow us to reach into the depths of our child and help him reach his full potential. A very difficult job indeed.

The sages waited a year because of an important idea in Jewish thought, the “endless spring of time”. Just like a spring circles constantly back to the same longitudinal point, but every time at a different latitudinal layer, the Jewish calendar circles around and around but each instance is capable of taking us up to a new level.

The sages waited to see if they could feel the energy of the previous year's events or not. If they could, it would become a holiday for the ages. If not, we could commemorate what was, but it wouldn't become the holiday Chanukah that we have today.

So, embedded in this time of year are unique spiritual energies that every one of us can tap into. And our great Rabbis gave us hints of what we can uniquely access at this time. One of those hints is in the name of the holiday.

Chanukah is a time that we can be mechanech, we can train into potential-utilization.

It is a wonderful thing that Jews around the world, bring their children, even from a very young age to light the candles and experience the mitzvah year after year.

But there is another layer we must address. What of our own potential? Can we use this time to be mechanech ourselves?

Actually, our sages say that after our parents finish their job, so to speak, it falls on us to be mechanech ourselves. Once we fly the nest, the responsibility of our own training falls squarely on our own shoulders.

This time is a beautiful time to train ourselves to connect. And Shabbos Chanukah, is a beautiful opportunity to connect to Hashem (our true purpose here in this world). Using a hammer to hold open a door, would be teaching the hammer to do something new. But using a hammer to bang in nails, is chinuch. When we deal with our physical needs, which we surely must do, that is teaching ourselves. But when we train ourselves into our true purpose of connecting to the infinite, that is chinuch.

May we see the beautiful light of Chanukah, and use it to be mechanech those around us at this time of year. But even more so, may we merit to be mechanech ourselves into the greatest purpose we have! Loving connection with Hashem!

A Happy Chanukah and a Great Shabbos!
