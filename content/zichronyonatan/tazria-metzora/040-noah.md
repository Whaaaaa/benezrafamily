In olden times, when the Jewish people as a whole stood on an exceedingly high spiritual level and G-d's presence was not so concealed, there was a unique affliction -- tzara'at -- that responded directly to a person's spiritual state, most famously to lashon hara, negative speech about others. The metzora was quarantined outside the camp, not primarily as punishment, but to give him the isolation needed to reflect on the damage careless words can do to a community.

In our current moment of literal quarantine, the parallel is impossible to ignore: an ancient framework built around isolation, purification, and gradual reintegration into community feels newly resonant. Perhaps our own quarantine, too, carries the same invitation -- not just to wait out a danger, but to use the isolation to reflect on how we speak about and treat one another, so that our reintegration into community is a genuine renewal.

Shabbat Shalom
