This week we finish our celebration of Sukkot, by celebrating Simchat Torah, "On the eighth day shall be a holy convocation to you… it is a holding back (atzeret)." A "holding back" is explained in a parable. A king throws a feast and many guests come, as guests begin to get full and head for the door the king holds back his closest friends. On this holiday we dance with the Torah, into the night.

The celebration of Simchat Torah is different than the celebration of Shavuot while both are to appreciate the value of Torah. On Shavuot we celebrate the Torah delivered on Mount Sinai, the tablets crushed by Moshe in response to the golden calf. We stay up all night with our community studying the nitty gritty details of cases, laws, and rituals so we do not make the mistakes of our ancestors. All of this to recognize the intrinsic value of learning Torah.

On Simchat Torah we celebrate a more personal Torah, after going through the high holidays and being forgiven for the golden calf Moshe descends with new tablets. Instead of the thunderous revelations of Sinai these tablets are delivered after Yom Kippur, when we are taken anew. After yom kippur we are again bound to our brothers, and embraced by the sukkah with our four species in hand. Hashem sees us having so much fun eating and walking to shul and shaking our lulavs he invites us to stay one more day. On Simchat Torah we appreciate that God took us from Egypt and gave us the Torah. We dance in circles because we know through the Torah God manipulates our lives on the most personal level.
