For twenty-two years, Yaakov mourned the loss of his beloved son Yosef, whom he assumed must have been devoured by a wild animal. Though so many years had passed, Yaakov simply could not be comforted or consoled – the wound was ever fresh and painful as the day Yosef had gone missing.

What is less well known is the spiritual repercussions that Yosef's disappearance had on Yaakov. He had been accustomed to frequent prophecy and the ecstasy of such closeness to the Almighty, but for those same twenty-two years Yaakov was altogether barred from divine revelation.

One reason for such is that a prerequisite to prophecy is joy; one who is deeply saddened is simply unable to muster up the joy required for the experience.

We can assume that being so distanced from Hashem was a personal tragedy of the highest proportion and was supremely agonizing for Yaakov, who breathed only for the will of his Creator. What's more, Yaakov had been previously told that if none of his children would die in his lifetime he would merit the world-to-come; conversely, if he were to lose a child he assumed he would lose his portion in the afterlife. This means that Yaakov thought for all those years that Hashem wanted nothing to do with him and that he would have no part in the next world. Effectively, Yaakov had lost all his dreams and aspirations and the entire purpose of his life.

On the topic of prophecy, we must know that one cannot become a prophet until they have scaled the highest heights and achieved the most lofty levels of perfection in mind and character. To make the cut, men of antiquity would spend a lifetime in preparation and achievement. If one faltered in his spiritual level, it could take months to regain the baseline requirements necessary to be visited by the Almighty. Perfection was essential at all times.

And yet, after twenty-two years of torment and the belief that he was unwanted by G-d, when Yaakov finally met up with Yosef, he immediately prophesied! As soon as the barrier of sadness had been lifted Yaakov was right there waiting, fully poised and perfected for divine revelation without missing a beat. Throughout all those terrible years, Yaakov maintained every ounce of personal achievement without giving up a single inch to the despair which would have destroyed a lesser man.

How did Yaakov accomplish such a superhuman act? What is the secret that empowered him to stand strong despite it all?

I think the answer may be a perception which can give all of us strength. Yaakov knew that when Hashem 'turns his back' it never means that He wants us to bug off and give up. When Hashem hides his face, it is because he wants us to redouble our efforts to connect. He wants us to rise up and overcome all obstacles in search of His countenance. He wants only to fortify our bond, replacing our run of the mill relationship with one that can go through fire and water unscathed and undiminished. If Hashem is hiding, He's waiting longingly for us to find him.

All of us go through times in our lives when things seem bleak, when we feel forsaken and forlorn; perhaps we even feel that G-d is uninterested in our relationship. At those times, we must remember the courage and fortitude of our father Yaakov. We must garner strength from his message that even when all seems lost we can hold our ground and continue to grow. And we can rest assured that Hashem is right there waiting to be found, waiting to be reunited with us, and waiting to shine His countenance upon us in a way we never could have imagined.

Good Shabbos,
