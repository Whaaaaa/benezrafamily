Nitzavim means "ones standing" -- in this week's Parsha, Bnai Yisrael is reminded of the unity of Israel, the future redemption, the practicality of Torah, and our freedom of choice, just as the High Holidays approach.

"You are all standing this day before the L-rd your G-d... your young children, your women, and your convert who is within your camp." The unity of Israel is bound not only by leadership but by familiar ties and shared labor -- relationships that help hold us accountable to our covenant.

Moshe warns of exile and destruction should we abandon that covenant, but also promises: "If your outcasts shall be at the ends of the heavens, from there will the L-rd your G-d gather you." Raging wildfires, hurricanes, and a global pandemic have destroyed so much this year, but our covenant with G-d remains intact -- and though we may be scattered, our interconnectivity has never been greater.

"For the mitzvah which I command you this day, it is not beyond you, nor is it remote from you... it is very close to you, in your mouth, in your heart, that you may do it." Torah is practical, earthly, close at hand -- an especially meaningful message before the High Holidays, when we may feel inadequate or unworthy.

Choosing Torah is as easy as choosing life: "I have set before you life and goodness, and death and evil... and you shall choose life." Soon we will gather with our communities and stand before G-d in judgment. May we all be inscribed in the book of life, blessed with a year of community, free of wildfire smoke, hurricane winds, and pandemic.
