Nitzavim means "Ones Standing" in this week's Parsha Bnai Yisroel is reminded of the unity of Israel, the future redemption, the practicality of Torah, and our freedom of choice. With the high holidays approaching, these verses remind us of our covenant with God.

"You are all standing this day before the Lord, your God the leaders of your tribes, your elders and your officers, every man of Israel. your young children, your women, and your convert who is within your camp both your woodcutters and your water drawers." The unity of Israel is bound not only by the authority of leaders but by familiar ties and working together. These relationships help hold us accountable to our covenant with God.

Moshe forwarns Bnai Yisrael of the exile and destruction that will grip us if we abandon our covenant. "You will return to the L-rd your God... If your outcasts shall be at the ends of the heavens, from there will the L-rd your G-d gather you . . . and bring you into the Land which your fathers have possessed." Raging wildfires, hurricanes and coronavirus have destroyed so much but our covenant with God is still intact. Though we may be scattered our interconnectivity is beyond that of any other era. We are responsible to fix for the destruction we see.

"For the Mitzvah which I command you this day, it is not beyond you, nor is it remote from you. It is not in heaven . . . It is not across the sea . . . Rather, it is very close to you, in your mouth, in your heart, that you may do it." The practicality of Torah is highlighted in this Parsha. This lesson is especially meaningful before the high holidays when we may feel inadequate or unworthy. Torah is an earthly endeavour for those who stand among us.

Choosing Torah is as easy as choosing life. "I have set before you life and goodness, and death and evil: in that I command you this day to love God, to walk in His ways and to keep His commandments . . . Life and death I have set before you, blessing and curse. And you shall choose life." Soon we will gather with our communities and stand before God in judgment, may we all be inscribed in the book of life and blessed with a year of community, a year free of wildfire smoke, hurricane winds and coronavirus. May we be blessed with the insight to see Torah everywhere so we may love God and choose life.
