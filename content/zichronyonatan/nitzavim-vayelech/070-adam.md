We are standing now at the edge of the world. The sages tell us that in every Shabbos is contained the entire potential of the week that follows -- and next Wednesday night will be Rosh Hashanah, the first day of 5778.

I recall when Uncle Yonatan was deported from Mexico to Arizona -- on Rosh Hashanah. Arizona called Colorado to say they had ten days to collect their prisoner, or he'd be released as no threat to anyone. They came exactly ten days later -- on Yom Kippur -- to pick him up. Uncle Yonatan said, "The timing is not lost upon my conscience."

Our Torah portion begins, "Atem Nitzavim Hayom Kulchem" -- "you are gathered here, all of you," to pass into a covenant with Hashem -- not only those physically present in the desert, but "those not gathered here, with us, today" -- every generation that follows. Rashi points out this "passing" is not passive but active, one that must be made by every individual, and yet also only realized when "all of you" come together.

Losing Uncle Yonatan was, by far, the hardest thing I have yet faced in this life -- and I realize it was decided last Rosh Hashanah, that just under a year ago, Hashem in His infinite wisdom decided to take a guiding light from all of us. And I am left with his words ringing in my ears: "not being lost upon my conscience."

So here I stand, on the edge of the world, thinking of the King I am about to face. And I see the relationship we are asked to pass into -- that every Jew, no matter where they are in life, has both the opportunity and the obligation to enter into this relationship with Hashem, to appreciate what we are given, and never let a message be "lost upon our conscience."

Uncle Yonatan taught us that the hardest things we encounter in life have meaning for us to receive -- and that we have a responsibility, to ourselves and to those around us, to search for that meaning even when we cannot understand it instantly.

Let us work to learn from Uncle, to make Hashem our King -- as individuals, but with the knowledge that only when individuals come together are nations formed. And in that merit may we make Hashem King over the entire world, with the coming of Moshiach, soon in our days!

Have a beautiful Shabbos and an amazing New Year! Love, Adam
