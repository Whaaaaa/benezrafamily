This week's Parsha, Emor, "Speak," focuses on laws regarding the Kohanim and the Temple service -- a Kohen may not become impure through contact with the dead except for close relatives; a Kohen Gadol may only marry a virgin; a Kohen with a physical deformity may not serve; a deformed animal may not be sacrificed.

The second half of the Parsha turns to the cycle of Jewish holidays: weekly Shabbat, Pesach and the counting of the Omer culminating in Shavuot, Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur ten days later, and Sukkot's seven days followed by Shemini Atzeret. The Parsha concludes with the story of a man executed for blasphemy, and laws of compensation for injury and property damage.

Two themes run through this Parsha: holiness and priestly purity on one hand, holidays and communal ceremony on the other -- together forming the foundation of Jewish community.
