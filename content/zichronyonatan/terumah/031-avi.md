This week's Parsha is Terumah, "offering." Bnai Yisrael is commanded to contribute fine materials -- gold, silver, copper, dyed wools, flax, animal hides, oil, spices, and gems -- to build the Mishkan, a dwelling place for Hashem. On Mount Sinai, Moshe was instructed on how to build it so it could be disassembled, carried, and reassembled with ease, so the Jewish people could carry it with them anywhere in the desert.

The two tablets, kept safe in the ark behind a woven curtain in the innermost chamber, represent our unique bond and communal experience at Sinai. In the outer chamber sat a seven-branched menorah and a table of showbread. Every structural detail -- the wood posts, the walls, the multi-layered roof, the sixty surrounding posts and linen tapestries -- was designed with intention. Not all design is aesthetic; the Mishkan teaches that the best projects are those built to involve an entire community and to meet that community where it is.

Design is not just what we see, but how we use and interact with things. We will never see the Mishkan with our own eyes, but by learning the principles behind its construction, we can see our own lives improve. Good Shabbos!
