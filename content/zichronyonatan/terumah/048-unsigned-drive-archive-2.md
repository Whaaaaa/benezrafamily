Terumah means lifted apart, this week in Meme's Parsha we learn about all the ways the Torah makes our lives more beautiful. God said to Moshe, "Make me a sanctuary and I will rest there"

The Parsha enumerates the materials and dimensions for the Mishkan: gold, silver and copper; blue-, purple- and red-dyed wool; flax, goat hair, animal skins, wood, olive oil, spices and gems were to be donated by each household based on their financial means.

Today we build and design our schools and sanctuaries in the image of the mishkan and holy temple. It is not practical today to take on such a feat of architecture however many synagogues are beautiful nonetheless, with balconies and stages some even have stained glass. More often of course synagogues are in a basement, or the sixth floor of an apartment complex. Some busses on the east coast are really like the mishkan, portable little sanctuaries.

The responsibility of having a library and place to pray falls on the community as a whole, if a community cannot afford to own a place they must rent a place. Conversely, If they have the means to own they must buy. The synagogue should be as beautiful as possible without having art that may appear to be idol worship, curtains and architectural embellishments are encouraged yet pictures of even a Rabbi may be inappropriate on some walls.

Ideally the synagogue would be the tallest building in the city, because today that is unreasonable some are careful not to buy a house taller than the synagogue they belong to. Beauty and aesthetics work hand in hand to create a synagogue. When designing a room we choose things for comfort and purpose. When designing a room for God Beauty is number one priority. Building a synagogue from scratch gives us the opportunity to permanently construct the halls, curtains, and barriers that direct our lives as Jews. Good Shabbos!
