"When the month of Adar enters, we increase our happiness!"
-The Sages of Blessed Memory

So Adar started this past Wednesday. The month in which arguably the happiest Holiday occurs, Purim. The holiday of dressing up, gift-giving, Tzedakah, festive meals, and singing and dancing until you're on the floor; what could be more happy?

But it's not enough to be happy on Purim, we are meant to be happy now, two weeks prior. Many point out that the wording increase implies we must always carry happiness but now is the time of abundance. The time to feel increasingly happy. The question is, how are we supposed to do this? What is meant to be happening that we should suddenly look at a calendar and start yodeling with joy?

The Sfas Emes explains that a cursory reading of the words of the sages will misguide us. One would assume the "entering" we refer to is the Jewish people entering the time-space of Adar. The Sfas Emes says we're actually talking about the month of Adar entering into us. It's not when we turn the calendar, but when internally, we bring Adar into us!

"Great! ...But how do I do that? What does it mean to bring Adar inside? How do I cause Adar to enter me and increase my happiness?"

The secret lies in a key juxtaposition of two encounters in the megillah.

The first is when Haman offers to buy the Jewish people for extermination at the price of 10,000 silver bars. This is an exorbitant sum of money from arguably the richest man alive at the time other than King Achashverosh himself.

The second is a discussion hiding in the Megillah. On his way to get Mordechai to parade him around the kingdom, Haman found Mordechai learning with thousands of children. He asked what Mordechai was learning and Mordechai responded, about the Mincha offering. The half-shekel that every Jew would give. At that moment, Haman realizes that the Jews half-shekel offering is going to win out against his 10,000 silver bars.

My Rebbe, Reb Moshe Weinberger says this is the secret to allowing Adar into our beings. Adar is all about the half-shekels. The way of Amalek and Haman, his descendant, was to create coldness by always looking toward the grandiose. 10,000 and it's all worth nothing unless every one of the Jewish people is exterminated.

But we fight with our half-shekel! Always focusing on the little things. When we bring the warmth of every little part of our day- every good morning, every smile, every bracha we make thinking about the words and relationship we have with the one above, every moment of remaining calm with our children, every time we stop and focus on our spouse with our full attention- all of the small things, we bring Adar into us. And when we live in the warmth of Yiddishkeit, we immediately become happy. When we allow Adar to enter us, we melt the coldness of Amalek with the warmth of a Judaism that sings to the one above!

And the beautiful thing is, it's always available. At every moment, there is a half-shekel to find. When we focus on each moment and what we can accomplish at that moment, we cause Adar to enter into us and become happier, more joyous, and more unified!

May we continue to grow together, one meaningful little step after another, until we all return home together, quickly in our days!
