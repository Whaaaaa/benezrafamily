I have seen the world turn upside down the past year perhaps more than any other time in my life. There may have been crazier personal battles and difficulties but to see the world flip and the increasing gap between head, heart, and action has been whiplashing.

More and more seems backwards and upside down and it's becoming increasingly difficult to gain clarity on issues from public health to economics and mental health to politics.

I ventured onto facebook for a couple of minutes today for the first time in months and felt uneasy the entire time. The more I read, the more of a sinking feeling I felt in my stomach. The difficulty for me is in seeing the emotional schism growing between family, friends and acquaintances. Gone are the times of civil discussion of disagreements. I am becoming very aware of the grip of each of our beliefs about life and searching for connection with loved ones across the divide of views and judgements about where we should be headed and even where we are, seems an increasingly dangerous option.

Enter this week's Parsha. No, not about guarding our mouths in these times, a bit further in we have a discussion about what to do when there are disagreements.

Read these words carefully: "If a matter of judgement is hidden from you… you shall go up to the place which Hashem your God has chosen… and to the judge who will be in your days… and you will do according to the ruling they tell you… and you shall be careful to do according to all they have told you… do not deviate from what they tell you right or left."

Rashi tells us: "in your days," even if the leaders in your generation are not what you had in previous generations, you must listen; you only have the leaders that are in your days.

He continues: "left or right," even if they tell you about right that it is left and about left that it is right.

I know this is the perfect opportunity for a political joke but I'll spare you.

Why would we listen to someone telling us the polar opposite of what we can clearly see with our own eyes?! Rashi didn't say "if they tell you that you made an egregious error and have the data backwards." He said "if they tell you left is right and right is left!"

I think there are deep secrets here both in understanding the Torah's view and in understanding each other.

Have you ever been on the phone giving directions to someone? "Turn right at the Walgreens," you say only to eventually realize they've driven ten minutes in the wrong direction. You've driven the route hundreds of times, you know the right way, but they were coming from the other direction. Right is only East if you're facing North.

Our perspective makes all the difference when we're trying to judge right and left. The thing is, it makes just as much difference when we're trying to judge right and wrong and true and false. Depending on where we're standing, the same information can mean a completely different thing.

I find this crucial when seeking to understand the position of anyone disagreeing with my judgment. I have to remember that everything they've done until now influences their view on the "data" and even influences where they will source their "data" from. They may have made a u-turn at some point in life which changed all future lefts to rights and vice versa. The weirder thing is, I might be the one turned around. Empirical truth becomes harder to verify and trust.

So aside from a healthy loving understanding that we might see the same facts from different angles resulting in different conclusions, we have two other tips I see in these verses right off the bat.

We can seek out the leaders "in our day" and we can always seek to be "going up to the place Hashem has chosen."

If we focus on our own growth as the goal, seek qualified leaders in our generation, and remember that we might be turned around, I think we can grow closer even with everything going on around us.

I love and miss you all so much,

Have an uplifting Shabbos,

Adam
