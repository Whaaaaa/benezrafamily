Did you ever hear of the great ladder which stands right here on earth but reaches into the highest heavens? Fleeing his brother's wrath toward the house of his uncle Lavan -- a man of great moral corruption -- Yaakov has a prophetic dream: a ladder rooted on earth, rising to the heavens, with angels ascending and descending.

Rabbi Shlomo Wolbe explains this dream teaches Yaakov, and all his descendants, that beyond performing mitzvot, we are charged with a further task: always climbing, always growing, never resting on our laurels. One might think this lesson belongs at life's beginning -- why does Yaakov, already sixty, need it now? Because entering Lavan's spiritually depraved house, it would have been easy to assume that merely maintaining his level was sufficient. The dream teaches: even here, even under duress, there is no such thing as staying put.

Moreover, it is specifically through difficulty that true growth is possible -- Yaakov reached his full spiritual greatness precisely by standing up to the tests in Lavan's house.

Uncle Yonatan personified this lesson. Throughout his life he faced countless challenges that would have broken a lesser man ten times over, yet he knew that in the face of pain, the only way to go is up -- that every test is an opportunity to strive higher. I pray we can learn his lesson and continue the path he set us on, growing through our challenges and never ceasing to reach for the stars.

Good Shabbos, Yosef
