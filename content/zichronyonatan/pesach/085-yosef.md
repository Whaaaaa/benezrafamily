Pesach is almost upon us and we're all running around like chickens without heads, frantically trying to get everything done in time. As we work so hard, and spend so much money, to celebrate Passover properly, it behooves us to tap into its power and take all we can from it.

I'd like to share a quick thought from Rabbi Dessler, of which Uncle Yonatan was very fond: in a spiritual sense, time does not flow as a line but as a circle, going around each year -- each chag carries the same power the original event had, so many years ago. Rosh Hashanah is intrinsically a time of renewal, Shavuot a time of connection to Torah, Pesach a time of freedom, and so on. During these times we can tap into their forces and experience similar events in our own lives.

Pesach, then, the time of our freedom, is a time when we can be set free from whatever shackles bind us -- bad habits, poor decisions, a rut in our personal or professional lives. This is an intrinsic element of these days, and we need only open our eyes to see its impact on our world. But it is in our hands how much we allow ourselves to be changed by it. True freedom is not the ability to do anything we wish, but the ability to rein in our desires and take control of our lives.

So this Pesach, as we sit like royalty at our seder tables, let us understand the power of the chag, see its repercussions in the world around us, and become free people -- free of our own limitations, able to choose our own direction in life.

Chag Sameach, Yosef
