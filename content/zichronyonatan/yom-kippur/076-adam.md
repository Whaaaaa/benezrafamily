We now stand, quite literally, on the brink of the world -- as the ten days between Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur reach their culmination. On Yom Kippur, the fast, the white garments, and the extra prayer of Ne'ilah as the gates begin to close all point toward a single truth: that teshuvah remains possible until the very last moment.

There is no more fitting place to end the Days of Awe than with the recognition that our fragility -- the very thing that terrifies us about standing in judgment -- is also what makes G-d's mercy meaningful. A judge who judges the perfect need not be merciful at all.
