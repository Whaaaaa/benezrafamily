The Mishnah in Sukkah tells us: a stolen or dried-out lulav is invalid; one from an idolatrous tree or a condemned city is invalid; one whose top is cut off is invalid... The specificity of these disqualifications, arriving right on the heels of Yom Kippur's account-settling, teaches that the joy of Sukkot cannot simply be declared -- it has to be built, plank by plank, out of scrupulous, specific attention to what is and isn't fit to bring before G-d.

Wishing everyone a Chag Sameach!
