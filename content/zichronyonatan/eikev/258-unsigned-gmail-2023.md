Parsha Eikev touches on some values that are truly at the core of Judaism. Eikev means “because” and opens with Moshe’s closing address to Bnai Yisroel. Moshe reiterates the promises of land and wealth God swore to our forefathers. Moshe continues with rebuke, reminding us of the sin of the golden calf, Korachs rebellion and the sin of the spies among other transgressions.

Moshe calls Bnai Yisroel rebellious yet in the same breath reminds them of Gods forgiveness. Moshe reminded them that for forty years God sustained us in the desert with manna and that man is not sustained on bread, but on the utterance of Gods mouth. Moshe describes Israel as flowing with milk and honey, and the center of divine providence. Moshe commands them to destroy the idols of foreign gods. God warns them that they cannot become full of themselves and believe that their weath and success is a broduct of their own strength and might.

A major passage that we recite daily is found in this Parsha, the second paragraph of Shema describes the rewards we receive when we follow Gods commandments. It also describes the adverse outcomes of not following commandments. Famine exile and supernatural disaster.

Once there was a troop of climbers who train for years in various conditions at higher and higher altitudes so that one day they can summit the highest peak. After years of training they embark on their journey, they walk and climp for months, the weather is severe but their gear protects them. One day they finally reach the peek and as they appreciate the awesome view a very small boy walks comfortablly out to them in his pajamas. Perplexed, the climbers ask the boy how he got there and the boy simply replies “ I was born here.”

It is easy to think that our accomplishments are ours and that our wealth is earned, and that we are deserving of the good that the world sends our way. But as Jews we know we are born on top of the mountain. We are imbued with a profound gratitude for our creator and we constantly remind ourselfs that our strength is given to us by God.
