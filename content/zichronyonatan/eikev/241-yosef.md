*And now, Israel, what does Hashem your G-d ask of you? Just to fear Hashem your G-d, to go in all his ways, to love him, and to serve him with all your heart and all your soul.*

This is a thoroughly perplexing verse. It is a short list of all the loftiest levels of spiritual achievement; things which one must strive his whole life to attain, and they are prefaced with the word 'just'?!

The Gemara *(Menachot 43b)* makes a seemingly unconnected *drash* from the beginning of the verse. Instead of reading מה, what, does Hashem ask, we read מאה – Hashem asks of you to make one hundred brochos, blessings, each and every day.

This is a very nice ideal on its own, but does it have anything to do with the rest of the verse?

Rabbi Avigdor Miller z"l teaches that it does. One hundred brochos is the way to achieve all those loftiest levels in the easiest way. In effect, מאה, meaning the hundred brochos, is what puts the מה, the ease, into all the holy achievements listed at the end. If one will only focus on the key of one hundred brochos, this will bring him quite easily to all the rest.

How so? Certainly not by hastily muttering a blessing under our breath while simultaneously stuffing the doughnut down our throat. And probably not by reciting Shmoneh Esrei while daydreaming about our summer plans either.

Rabbi Miller explains that brochos are really a portal into living with Hashem at all times. When making a blessing on a delicious fruit, or a scrumptious baked good, or a savory steak, we are ascribing all of our enjoyment to Hashem. When we daven, beseeching Hashem for all of our greatest needs and deepest desires, we are affirming that every facet of our life depends on, and is being sponsored by Hashem. When we make a blessing after using the facilities, we recognize the great miracles of our bodily systems and, again, we recognize that it is all the kindness of Hashem.

But this is just the beginning.

As we continue to make brochos and think about what we are saying, truly thanking and asking Hashem for everything, we are becoming new people. We are becoming thankful people. We are training ourselves not to take everything for granted, but to be aware of every good thing which we enjoy.

Let's take an apple for example. Yesterday, we ate an apple with no thought at all, mumbling a quick brocha and devouring our snack. Today however, we made a thoughtful brocha, thanking Hashem for the sweet-tasting, healthy apple. The next day, as we become more appreciative, we realize another dimension. Wow, it's not just delicious and nutritious, it even has that satisfying crunch. And so on. We notice that, in addition, it's beautiful! And it's the perfect size for my hand. It comes ready-made, right off the tree! It's got this incredible protective wrapper (the peel) which keeps it fresh and juicy! Hey, and you can even eat the wrapper! And the more we work on our blessings, on being thankful, we find more and more facets to every pleasure in the world. Our apple eating experience has transformed. Besides providing us with loads more enjoyment than it previously did, it has opened our eyes and our hearts to the love and kindness of our creator. Our relationship with Hashem has begun a new chapter.

And it's not just apples. There are hundreds, no, thousands of such kindnesses which we take for granted every day. Think what life would be like if we appreciated every breath of air. Every movement of our limbs. Every chirp of a bird, every flower, every cool breeze. Each of these things consists of a million miracles, each is a manifestation of endless kindness from above.

If we would just realize these things we'd never have a bad day in our lives. And if just a hundred of these miracles would elicit a heartfelt 'thank you' to Hashem, we would be elevated, almost automatically, to untold heights.

To fear Hashem? With such a clear perception of His limitless power, and our dependence on Him for so many miracles, how could we not fear Him?

To love him? Is there any more natural feeling for one who bestows such goodness upon you?

To go in his ways and to serve him with all our heart and soul? As we marvel at every facet of his creation all day every day, aren't we compelled to act in his glorious ways and serve him to the very best of our abilities?

So let's make an effort to unlock the secret of one hundred brochos. There's no better investment for this world and for the next.

*Yosef*
