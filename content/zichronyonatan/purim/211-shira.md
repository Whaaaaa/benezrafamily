Here is a little thought for you to enjoy.

When the evil Haman was trying to convince King Achashverosh to grant him permission to kill the Jewish People he made the following argument: "There is a nation that is scattered and separated."

One may wonder what the difference is between the two adjectives. Are they not one and the same?

I recently heard an interesting explanation. Scattered is referring to their physical location. They literally live all over the kingdom. But separated is describing the overall emotional state. Although they are all belonging to the same people and religion, they feel separated from one another. They don't see each other as equals or as having things in common with them. They may be next-door neighbors, pray in the same synagogue, and send their children to the same schools, but they are aloof and judge one another and hold themselves apart.

King Achashverosh hands over his signet ring to Haman and says that he may do with it as he pleases.

Sure enough, letters go out to every corner of the kingdom informing them of a special day that would approach when they would be allowed to kill all of the Jews. The Jews began fasting and crying and praying and through the righteous Queen Esther, they received salvation.

The day that they were going to be killed became a day of celebration instead. Jews gave baskets of food one to the other. No matter how they had felt about their neighbors and fellow Jews before the terrible decree, they now felt very united and as one.

We may live on opposite sides of the globe but still feel very close to one another and accepting of them in our hearts. On the other hand, we may have people in our own homes who we judge and look down upon. There is no greater pain than feeling separate or other than. We may not always agree with others, but we should never feel separate in our hearts.

This year, may we merit to feel close and as one with our fellow humans so we may merit to have salvation once again.

Have a wonderful Purim.

-Shira
