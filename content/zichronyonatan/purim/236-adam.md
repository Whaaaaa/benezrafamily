Nesivos Shalom asks an important question that we may have missed as we read the Megillah year after year. It's easy to get caught up in the success of the underdog and the beautiful way that every move the "bad guys" make is suddenly flipped to create their own destruction. How satisfying it is! But I'm out excitement, we must slow down and ask, how on earth could Mordechai endanger the entire Jewish nation by not bowing down to the viceroy of the king? This wasn't his own skin he was risking; every man, woman, and child would be at risk for playing with the ego of a tyrant! Does the Torah not have anything to say in such a case?! Aren't we meant to exercise caution in such scenarios?!

To understand we have to open our hearts. Mordechai was a prophet. He knew full well that the Jewish people were already written and sealed for destruction in the heavenly tribunal. He knew that the case was closed; there was no longer room to argue or submit any more evidence to the court. The Jewish people had been sealed for destruction.

AND Mordechai also knew that the power of true, real, deep-seated trust in the almighty was exactly the ticket out. As a 2-year-old jumping from a high place toward the back of a parent absorbed in other things, with certainty far outside the natural order that he will safely land in his mother or father's arms, Mordechai knew we would be saved. But he knew we would be saved for the sheer fact that we placed our complete trust in the almighty. So if he would bow, submitting to the natural order of things, we wouldn't stand a chance. But if Mordechai placed his utter and complete trust in the infinite Source of all existence, the Jewish people would be saved with miracle after miracle.

The Slonimer Rebbe, author of the Nesivos Shalom points out as well that the fast is called the fast of Esther not of Mordechai. His trust was so strong that fasting was not necessary. He submitted to all that Esther asked but knew that none of that would yield results. Only complete trust in the Source would save us. Mordechai even said as much to Esther, "And if not, expansiveness and salvation will arise for the Jewish people from another place and you and your father's house will be lost."

Any time we reach a pillar in time of a certain character trait that underlies the holiday, there is a unique opportunity for us to tap into and gain further perfection within that specific area. As we look around a world that is increasingly terrifying from a lower perspective, may we all merit to tap into the deepest sense of trust in our Creator, and the Sustainer of entire worlds. May we have the wisdom to know when it is time for us to act and also to know that our actions do not truly yield the results we seek. May we reach the level of Mordechai and Esther and through our trust and connection to the united shared consciousness with the Oneness of all, may we merit the full and complete redemption of the entire Jewish people and the entire world, soon in our days!

--Adam
