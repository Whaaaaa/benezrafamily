In this week's Parsha, "Shemot," or "Names," Moshe is born. As the children of Israel multiply in Egypt, Pharaoh, seeing them as a threat, enslaves them and orders Hebrew infants drowned in the Nile. Moshe's parents, Yocheved and Amram, try to conceal their radiant newborn, and finally set him afloat in a waterproof basket, watched over by his sister Miriam.

Pharaoh's daughter finds the baby and raises Moshe in the palace as an Egyptian. As a young man, witnessing the brutality of slavery, Moshe kills an Egyptian taskmaster beating a Jewish slave -- and must flee to Midian when the act becomes known, eventually becoming a shepherd for his father-in-law.

At the base of Mount Sinai, Moshe encounters the burning bush, where G-d reveals Himself and commissions Moshe to free his people. Nervous to speak, Moshe asks that Aharon serve as his spokesman. When Moshe and Aharon first demand the people's freedom, Pharaoh only increases their workload, and Moshe cries out, "Why have You done evil to these people?" G-d promises that redemption is close at hand.
