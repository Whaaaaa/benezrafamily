After a 34-year stint away from home, Yaakov finally decides to return to the land of Canaan. He remains fearful of a confrontation with his brother Esav, and sends angels ahead to prepare the way, before wrestling through the night with a mysterious stranger -- understood by our sages to be Esav's guardian angel. Yaakov emerges from the struggle wounded but victorious, renamed Yisrael: one who has striven with the Divine and with man, and prevailed.

The lesson of this wrestling match is that our greatest spiritual victories often come not despite our wounds, but through them -- the limp Yaakov carries afterward is not a defeat but a badge of the struggle that transformed him.
