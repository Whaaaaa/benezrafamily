At the beginning of this week's Parsha, Vayishlach, Jacob sends angels to Esau in hope of reconciliation, but the messengers report Esau is coming with 400 armed men. Jacob encounters the angel, the spirit of Esau, and wrestles with him and dislocates his hip. Jacob defeats the angel who renames him Israel which means "he who prevails over the divine".

The Torah does not fully change Jacob's name to Israel, but both Jacob and Israel are now used to refer to Jacob. As the father of the 12 tribes, Jacob contained within him the entire Jewish nation, therefore his ancestry is vital to the Jewish nation. It is said, “In the quest to create a dwelling place for G‑d in this world, a Jew can operate in two ways: as Jacob or as Israel”. The word Jacob represents a Jew’s service while hindered by the world. Israel refers to a jew who serves G-d in a way that allows G-dliness to be revealed for all to see. Jacob started with the name Jacob because the name represents the way a Jew begins service to G-d. Only after he triumphed over Esau’s angel and returned to the land of Israel did he get the elevated status of Israel. The Sages say that naming a baby is a statement of her character, her specialness, and her path in life. For at the beginning of life we give a name, and at the end of life a "good name" is all we take with us. May we
all strive to live up to our names.
