In this week's Parsha Yaakov sends messengers to his brother Esav ahead of his return to Israel. Though sent to make peace, the messengers return with news that Esav is approaching with an army of 400 men. Yaakov prepares three ways: for war, in prayer, and with a gift of hundreds of livestock.

That night, Yaakov moves his family across the river but stays behind, where he wrestles until dawn with a man our sages identify as Esav's angel. Though injured, Yaakov overcomes the angel and receives a blessing -- and a new name, Yisrael, "he who prevails over the Divine."

The next morning Esav meets Yaakov and embraces him; he tries to refuse the gift but Yaakov insists. Esav invites Yaakov to Seir, but Yaakov politely declines. Passing through Shechem, Yaakov's daughter Dinah is taken and violated by the local prince; her brothers Shimon and Levi respond with force against the city.

Yaakov is troubled by his sons' violence and does not condone it. He learns of his mother's death, and as he rushes home his wife Rachel gives birth to Binyamin -- but dies shortly after, buried in Beit Lechem. When Yaakov finally arrives home, his father Yitzchak passes away at 180. The Parsha ends with a list of Esav's lineage.

Good Shabbos!
