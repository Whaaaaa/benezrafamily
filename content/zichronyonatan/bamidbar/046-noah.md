When Moshe ascended Mount Sinai to receive the Torah, he was met with fierce opposition -- the angels made an uproar: "What is mortal man doing here?" These angels believed the Torah belonged in the heavens, not in fallible human hands. Bamidbar opens with a census of the Jewish people in the desert -- and there is a profound connection between the opposition Moshe faced at Sinai and the counting of a nation that would go on to carry that Torah, in all its human fallibility, through history.
