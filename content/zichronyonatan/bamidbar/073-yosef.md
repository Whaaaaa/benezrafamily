While in the desert, Hashem commanded Moshe multiple times to take a census of the people. While this might be an understandable process for a mortal government, it is quite perplexing for a people led by Hashem and His prophet Moshe. Hashem, of course, knows every single person down to his most minute details, and he certainly knew how many they numbered, so what was the purpose of counting them?

The Ramban explains that the census was indeed not for the sake of those counting, but for the sake of the people being counted. As the people heard the final count -- over six-hundred-thousand battle-ready men, not to mention the women, youngsters, and elders -- they were astounded at the number. They remembered how, just a few generations back, their ancestors had arrived in Egypt with a mere seventy people. Now, as they bore witness to the tremendous proliferation with which Hashem had blessed them, they were overcome with happiness, gratitude, and the realization of Hashem's great love for them.

This was the purpose of the census; not so much to count the people as to get the people to count their blessings.

And this is the lesson that the census holds for all of us. Every person, no matter his situation, has an endless list of blessings to count. Rabbi Avigdor Miller used to say that if a person would know how many miracles take place when he just uses the facilities, he would write home each time to let his family know that the procedure was successful. And so it is with every breath of air, every step, and every meal. These things are well-known to be miracles of 'nature', but we tend to take them for granted, never stopping for a moment to count the blessings. But, if we would just get into the habit of thinking about the little things, we would find endless lists of things to be thankful for.

Such a habit would not only help us achieve perfection of character and the greatness of gratitude. It would not only help us build a beautiful and fulfilling relationship with Hashem. But it would start us on the path to a life brimming with happiness. A person who appreciates the myriad little miracles of life would never have a bad day, would never let life's inconveniences and troubles get him down, and would feel forever like the luckiest man alive.

No price would be too high for such a life of happiness, so what are we waiting for? Right away, let's learn the lesson of the census and learn to count every blessing. I guarantee that we won't regret it.

Good Shabbos,

Yosef
