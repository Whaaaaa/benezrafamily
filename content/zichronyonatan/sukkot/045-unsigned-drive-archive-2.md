*"And there arose not since a prophet in Israel like Moses, whom G-d knew face to face; [who performed] all the signs and wonders which G-d sent [Moses] to do in the land of Egypt... [who equaled] that mighty hand, those great awesome things, which Moses did before the eyes of all Israel."* These are some of the Torah's final remarks before it is rewound and its cyclical ascent into Sukkot and Simchat Torah is celebrated, here at basecamp 5778. Rashi comments that *"those great awesome things, which Moses did before the eyes of all Israel" -* is actually a reference to the splitting of the tablets at the event of the golden calf.

This begs the question, to Hashem which takes precedence, the torah or the jewish people? Are people created in order to carry out the Torah or is the Torah here to enrich our lives? Or are they an indivisible whole? When Moshe came down from Har Sinai with tablets in hand and witnessed the golden calf, options lay before him, decisions, opportunities to make an example and set the record straight. In that split moment when Moshe threw down the tablets he made a decision to bind the jewish people to our commitment to Torah by making them intrinsically reliant upon eachother. If Moshe would have delivered the tablets at the event of the golden calf the burden of being a Jew alone would have killed all of us. This does prove our separation from gentiles greater, even in the middle of our midnight mutiny extravaganza, Torah in the most literal sense saves us. If we are not up to snuff the Torah is in a million pieces, this infers the value of the soul to endure over the Torah.

Of course without the Torah and this pinnacle decision we would not have a base for assessing the value of human life, making the Torah a key with many corresponding locks, and the jewish people a lock without key.

They are two sides of the same coin the soul cannot be fully harnessed without Torah and Torah cannot be fathomed to its depths until souls search it and observe it as shattered. Therefore, the ultimate Torah was created at the moment our input was received, when fate was changed because we woke up late. The ultimate Torah is the one we study and tweek, teach and discover, read and yell. The torah that is made by interactions, the Torah we wear, eat, in the Torah we go.

Everything we are and receive is in "quantized packages". Truth blessings and wisdom are infinite but we are not. Like Hansel and Gretel we are lucky to find morsels carving out our winding narrow path. Even the tablets were quantized, ten commandments if you do them great, if not then not. When Hashem saw Moshe mourning the tablets it states, "You have done well by smashing the tablets. For now you will receive a Torah that you may extend wider than the sea." With this second tablets came a platform for the jewish people to manipulate the torah from the inside, the Oral Torah.

If there is one thing sure to put you ahead in life, it's failure, it's exactly like success only instead of leading to wealth and wholeness, with failure you shrink. You fall inside yourself and see yourself in shattered disarray and think this will not make due, I'm leaving! Now you escape and join the infinite and discover what was there the whole time. Both the broken and complete tablets were placed in the arc, so to in your heart there is a set of each. When you find the infinite where will you put it? Your regular vessel won't hold it and neither will the the broken one. This is the nature of humans that trumps angels we can exist fully here and partially there we embody the dualities of the broken and whole. That is why it is our responsibility to make the invisible visible and bring heaven to earth.

*The learning should be a merit for Yonatan ben Avraham Avinu a"h*
