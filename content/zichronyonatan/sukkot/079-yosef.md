The Mishnah in Succah tells us: a stolen or dried lulav is invalid; one from a tree used for idol worship or from a condemned city is invalid; one with a broken top is invalid -- an extraordinary level of specificity for a single mitzvah performed just once a year, for a few brief moments, waved in six directions.

The depth of this specificity teaches that joy on Sukkot -- called by the sages "the time of our happiness" -- is not simply declared into being. It is built the way a home is built: plank by plank, law by law, out of scrupulous care for what is fit to bring before G-d and what is not. A joy assembled with this much care is not fragile; it can hold an entire holiday, and an entire people, inside it.

Wishing everyone a Chag Sameach!
