"And there arose not since a prophet in Israel like Moses..." These are among the Torah's final words before it is rewound and its cyclical ascent into Sukkot and Simchat Torah begins again, here at basecamp 5778. Rashi comments that "those great awesome things which Moses did before the eyes of all Israel" refers to the shattering of the tablets at the sin of the golden calf.

To Hashem, which takes precedence -- the Torah or the Jewish people? When Moshe descended and saw the golden calf, he made a decision: by shattering the tablets, he bound the Jewish people to their commitment to Torah by making it intrinsically dependent on them. Had he delivered the tablets intact at that moment, the burden of being a Jew, unmediated by our own frailty, might have destroyed us. The Torah is a key with many corresponding locks; the Jewish people are a lock without a key -- neither complete without the other.

The soul cannot be fully harnessed without Torah, and Torah cannot be fathomed to its depths until souls search it and, in some sense, shatter it in the searching. The ultimate Torah is not a static object but the one we study, tweak, teach, discover, wear, and eat -- made real through interaction.

Everything we receive comes in "quantized packages" -- truth and blessing are infinite, but we are not. Like Hansel and Gretel, we find morsels that carve our winding, narrow path. Even the tablets were quantized -- ten commandments, complete in themselves. Yet Hashem tells Moshe, "You have done well by smashing the tablets. Now you will receive a Torah you may extend wider than the sea" -- and with the second tablets comes the Oral Torah, a platform for us to manipulate and extend Torah from within.

If there is one thing sure to move you forward in life, it's failure -- exactly like success, only instead of wealth, it shrinks you, and you see yourself in shattered disarray. But both the broken and the whole tablets were placed together in the ark -- so too in your heart there is room for both. This is what distinguishes humans from angels: we can exist fully here and partially there, embodying the duality of broken and whole. That is why it falls to us to make the invisible visible, and to bring heaven down to earth.

The learning should be a merit for Yonatan ben Avraham Avinu a"h.
