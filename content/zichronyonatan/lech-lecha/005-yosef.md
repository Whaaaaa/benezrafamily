Our forefather Avraham was given ten great tests in his lifetime. These were the defining moments in his life and served as the catalysts for his becoming the father of the entire Jewish nation. In the beginning of Parshat Lech Lecha we find one of these tests: the test to leave his family and homeland to travel to the land of Israel.

Yet it is curious that when commanded to set out, Avraham was guaranteed by Hashem that it would be to his benefit -- children, wealth, honor. So what exactly was the test? Rabbi Moshe Feinstein explains that the test was not about the journey itself, but about the mindset: Avraham knew G-d could do anything, so why should he have to travel so far to receive blessings He could give right where he stood? The test was to accept the decree of the Almighty without asking questions -- to realize that G-d has a master plan we are not meant to fully understand.

This is the greatness of Avraham, and it behooves all of us to incorporate this attribute into our daily lives. We often convince ourselves that the only way we can be happy is if we get a particular thing, and when we don't receive it we feel frustration, sadness, even anger. We are charged with the task of tamim tihyeh im Hashem Elokeichem, "you shall be wholehearted with G-d" -- to trust that He knows what is for our ultimate good far better than we ever could.

If we can emulate Avraham's ways, then we are sure to live a life of endless happiness, peace of mind, and security -- with G-d's help.
