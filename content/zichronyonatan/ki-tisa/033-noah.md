This week's Parsha begins with the commandment to take a census of Bnei Yisrael -- counting each person by collecting a half-shekel rather than counting individuals directly, teaching that a Jew is never counted merely as a number, and that our merit before G-d comes bound up with our community.

The Parsha also contains the sin of the Golden Calf and Moshe's intercession on the people's behalf -- shattering the first tablets in the moment he sees the calf, and later receiving the second set, engraved not by G-d's hand alone but through partnership with Moshe's own effort. The second luchot, unlike the first, come through a relationship rebuilt after failure -- perhaps a deeper, more enduring foundation than the first perfection ever could have been.

Shabbat Shalom
