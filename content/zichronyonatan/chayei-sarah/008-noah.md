When a person arrives in a new town and is looking for a kosher place to eat, it becomes readily apparent just how seriously he takes his own standards. This week's parsha, Chayei Sarah, is fittingly Uncle Yonatan's own bar mitzvah parsha, and it opens -- somewhat paradoxically -- with the death of Sarah rather than the "life" its name suggests.

Sarah spent a lifetime traveling and spreading the word of monotheism at Avraham's side, through years of infertility and captivity. Rashi teaches that the years of her life were "equal in goodness" -- meaning not that every year was pleasant, but that she met every year, pleasant or not, with the same steady goodness of character.

Avraham purchases Ma'arat HaMachpela -- the first act of Jewish land acquisition in Israel, turning a divine promise into physical reality. Eliezer is sent to find a wife for Yitzchak, and Rivka is identified through her spontaneous kindness at the well -- proving that character, not circumstance, marks the continuation of Sarah's legacy.

May we all merit to meet our own years -- the easy ones and the difficult ones alike -- with the same steady goodness that made Sarah's life whole.

Shabbat Shalom
