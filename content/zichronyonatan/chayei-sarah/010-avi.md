This week's Parsha Chayei Sarah opens by recounting the years of Sarah's life -- 127 years -- and her burial in the cave of Machpelah, purchased by Avraham from Ephron the Hittite for four hundred silver shekels. The Torah repeats "the years of Sarah's life," and Rashi teaches the repetition comes to show us that all her years were "equal in goodness."

Eliezer is sent to Charan to find a wife for Yitzchak, with the sign being a woman who offers to water his camels. Rivka does so, is invited back to Canaan, and marries Yitzchak, who is comforted from the loss of his mother. Avraham takes a new wife, Hagar, and fathers six more children before dying at 175, buried beside Sarah by both his sons, Yitzchak and Yishmael.

This week's Parsha is Uncle Yonaton's Parsha. Uncle prepared me and the rest of us for a world without him. By instilling a love of lifelong learning and Jewish practice in all of his children, Uncle Yonaton made sure the Torah he brought to our family continues to grow here forever.

Good Shabbos! -- Avi
