As the Israelites marched triumphantly out of Egypt, still giddy with freedom, they found themselves between a rock and a hard place. Behind them, they were pursued by the might and majesty of the Egyptian army, while in front of them loomed the impenetrable Sea of Reeds. Chaos ruled.

Sensing the impossibility of the situation, our leader Moshe took the only sensible course of action, engaging in fervent prayer to the Creator. Just what you would expect him to do. But Hashem didn't approve of this decision, and responded with some very cryptic words. "Why are you crying out to me? Speak to the Children of Israel and tell them to travel."

The Ohr Hachaim formally asks the questions that must bother all of us. Firstly, what should a Jew do in a time of need but cry out to Hashem? And second, where exactly should they travel with the sea just as wet as ever?

The Ohr Hachaim answers with a life-altering lesson. He says that to merit a miracle, one must be vindicated by the divine trait of mercy. If there is an accusation against him which does not allow a merciful judgment, prayer alone cannot make one deserving of the miraculous.

At that time, there was a great heavenly accusation being leveled at the Jewish people. The Jews had worshiped idols in Egypt just like the Egyptians, so why were they deserving of victory at the enemy's expense. Because of this charge, prayer was of no use – they simply did not deserve to be saved.

What then is one's recourse? They must take the next step themselves, throwing their lot in with G-d. When they act out of absolute faith in Hashem, by relying only on Him, that faith itself can increase their merits and make them deserving of salvation.

This was Hashem's message to Moshe. There is no room for prayer to improve your situation; therefore, you must tell the people to march headlong into the water, relying on Me to save them. And that is exactly what happened. Nachshon the son of Aminadav heroically took charge and walked confidently into the water until it reached his nose – when Hashem saw the faith which the Jews placed in Him, he split the sea and smote their enemies.

Each of us face challenges in life which seem insurmountable. We find ourselves with enemies on all sides and no route of escape. Hopeless. Sometimes we even cry out to G-d, but to no avail. At times like these, we need to learn this lesson, and take a step in the right direction, even if it seems like it's into a brick wall. We have to trust in Hashem to fill in where we fall short, and in this way we can merit our own splitting of the sea.
