This weeks Parsha is Pinchas, it is a difficult Parsha that deals with conflict between Jews and the influence of Midianites. It also includes laws regarding the division of land among the tribes and within a family. Moshe officially appoints Yehoshua as leader after his death and God commands israel to offer extra sacrifices on Shabbat and Yom Tov.

Pinchas is a difficult figure, he is rewarded for publicly murdering a head of tribe who was making a skeptikal of his affair with his Midianite paramour. God blesses him with a covenant of peace and priesthood. This is a fitting blessing that reminds us to value peace overall.

A census is conducted and 601,730 men between twenty and sixty were counted. Moshe is instructed on how to divide land between tribes and within families. The daughters of Tzelaphchad appeal to Moshe. As daughters of a man with no son they had nolrgal heir from their generation. Moshe heard their plight and incorporated their exception into the Torahs laws of inheritance.

Moshe tasks Joshua with leading the Jews into Israel. The Parsha ends with a list of all the Musaf offerings brought on Shabbat, Rosh Chodesh (first of the month), and the festivals of Passover, Shavuot, Rosh Hashana, Yom Kippur, Sukkot and Shemini Atzeret.

Shabbat Shalom!
