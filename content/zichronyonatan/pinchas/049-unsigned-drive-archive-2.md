In this weeks Parsha Moshe appointed his successor, Yehoshua Bin Nun. While he is certainly a great person, what were the outstanding characteristics that allowed him to fill such an important role?

The role of a leader is to ignite people and to keep their passion for life and learning burning strong. The most important way to achieve this is through consistency. Just like a pot of water on the stove if removed and returned will never come to a boil, we cannot grow and become greater people without constant dedication.

About Yehoshua, it is written that he was "A lad, who did not depart from within the tent." Even from his youth, he knew that to achieve greatness he would have to dedicate himself constantly to learning. He never took breaks or vacations and soon was burning brighter than any of Moshe's other students.

We see this same value of consistency reflected in the korban tamid, the daily offering. It was responsible for atoning for all of our sins. This is because a consistent effort can overcome any odds.

Yehoshua, like Moshe, would have to lead his people to new heights and inspire them to persevere through trying times. Using his character of consistency, Yehoshua would be able to accomplish this massive endeavor.
