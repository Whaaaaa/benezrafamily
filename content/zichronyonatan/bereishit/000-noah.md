I once heard a story of a seemingly regular older woman who was walking with her grandchild when suddenly a car spun out of control. In the chaos that followed, onlookers were stunned by her composure and her insistence that every life in that car -- friend or stranger -- mattered as much as her own grandchild's. When asked how she could react this way, she explained simply: every person carries a piece of G-d within them, and that piece is worth protecting no matter whose face it wears.

This week we begin the Torah anew with the story of Creation, and specifically the creation of Adam, formed from dust and animated with G-d's own breath. Chazal teach that a single human being was created alone -- not a tribe, not a nation -- so that no person could ever say "my ancestor is greater than yours." Each of us descends from the same single person, each of us carries the same divine breath.

This is the foundational idea of Bereishit: that every human life carries within it a piece of the Creator Himself, and that this truth undergirds every mitzvah between one person and another that will follow across the rest of the Torah. As we begin the cycle again, may we merit to see that piece of G-d in everyone we encounter.

Shabbat Shalom
