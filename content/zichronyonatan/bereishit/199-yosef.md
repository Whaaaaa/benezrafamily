We all know that on the seventh day of creation G-d created rest. But what does it mean to create rest? Isn't rest just a lack of activity; not a proactive creation unto itself?

The Beis Halevi (1820-1892) explains that the way things seem to the naked eye is really a mistake. We think that for six days there was creation and afterwards it was finished. Now it seems as if things just go by themselves, running off of their original creation so many years ago. This, says the Beis Halevi, is a fundamental misconception. In truth, Hashem is constantly creating the entire world anew, to the point that if he would just stop creating for one second, the entire universe would cease into oblivion. It wouldn't need to be destroyed - it would simply come apart at the seams.

According to this understanding, nothing less was created on Shabbos than on the rest of the days of creation. Even on Shabbos everything was created anew for every second of the day. If so the term 'rest' refers not to a lack of creation but to the recreation of things exactly as they were before. So yes, 'rest' required creation just like all other facets of the world.

And this is one of the central messages of Shabbos, the day commemorating the constant recreation of the universe,. To understand that nothing is running on auto-pilot. It is on the Shabbos that we remember and realize that Hashem is perpetually and persistently involved in our world. He does not play a backseat roll, but is always running the entire show.

In this light, the world we live in takes on a whole new significance. When we walk out in the morning and see the beauty of a new day – the rising sun, the music of the birds, the leaves rustling in the breeze – it is our loving father making it all for our sake. When we face a challenge – a difficult person, a concern about our livelihood, a problem with our health – it is again our loving father orchestrating it all for our ultimate benefit. And so it is with every facet of the world which we live in; G-d doesn't have a cool and passive relationship with us, but a constant and intimate connection which weaves itself into even the most minute details.

So let us just realize his persence, feel his love and involvement, and reciprocate with an effort to build our bond to the Almighty from our side of the fence as well.

Good Shabbos,

Yosef
